Acceleration is defined as the rate of change of velocity over time. Use the formula \(a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}\), where \(\Delta v\) is the change in velocity and \(\Delta t\) is the change in time. Ensure all units are in a consistent system (m/s for SI or ft/s for USCS) before applying this formula. For unit conversion in SI, use dimensional analysis. For example, to convert km/h to m/s, set up the ratio: \( \frac{1000\, \text{m}}{1\, \text{km}} \times \frac{1\, \text{hour}}{3600\, \text{s}} \). In USCS, to convert miles/h to ft/s, use: \( \frac{5280\, \text{ft}}{1\, \text{mile}} \times \frac{1\, \text{hour}}{3600\, \text{s}} \).
