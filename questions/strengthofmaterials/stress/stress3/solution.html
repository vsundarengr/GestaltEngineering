The first step is to draw a free-body diagram of all the forces acting on the weight. There are only three forces, two tension forces are pointing in the direction of $R_1$ and $R_2$ away from the weight. There is a weight force point straight down. Using the free-body diagram compute the tension forces.
    Computing the tension forces:

    Using the FBD and the static equilibrium equation.

  • $\Sigma{F}_y = 0 ~\implies T_1sin(\theta1) + T_2sin(\theta_2)-W = 0$

  • $\Sigma{F}_x = 0 ~\implies T_2cos(\theta_2) - T_1cos(\theta_1)=0$

  • $T_2 = \frac{W}{\frac{cos(\theta_2)sin(\theta_1)}{cos(\theta_1)}+sin(\theta_2)}$
  • $T_1 = \frac{T_2cos(\theta_2)}{cos(\theta_1)}$
