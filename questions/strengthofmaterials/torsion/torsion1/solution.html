Compute the net torque that will be acting on the cross-section of AA. The torque acting on the cross-section of AA is simply $ \tau_1$ or $ \tau_2 - \tau_3$ = ${{params.t1}}$. Now you can use this value to calculate the shear stress.

    The equation to calculate the shear stress is $ shear stress = \frac{\tau ~ C}{J}$

    Where J = $\frac{\pi}{2} ~ R^4$

    In solving for the shear stress on the surface of the beam C is equal to the radius of the beam.

    So solution for the first question becomes: $ \frac{\tau_1 ~ 2}{\pi ~ R^3}$

    Be sure to convert to SI before calculating.

    For the second part, since we are no longer solving for shear stress at the surface of the beam C does not equal the radius

    C = ${{params.r}}$. So our solution now becomes: $ \frac{\tau_1 ~ 2 ~ C}{\pi R^4} $

  • Shear stress ${{params.r}} ~{{params.unitsDistt}}$ way from the axis= ${{correct_answers.shear2}}$ Pa
