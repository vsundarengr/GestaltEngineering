The first step is to compute the net torque acting about the pipe due to the forces. The torque acting on the pipe is $\tau = F_1(L_1) + F_2 (L_2)$. Now with the torque, you are able to use the shear stress equation.
  • Shear stress = $ \frac{\tau ~ C}{J}$
  • Where J = $\frac{\pi}{2} (R^4)$. Or for a pipe J = $\frac{\pi}{2} (R_{out}^4-R_{in}^4)$

    • Computing shear stress for the outer wall:
