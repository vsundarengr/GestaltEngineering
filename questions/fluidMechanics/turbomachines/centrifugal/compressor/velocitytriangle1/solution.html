Since the entry is radial, the inlet triangle is a right triangle. By continuity, the flow rate at the inner radius (entry) and the flow rate at the outer radius (exit) is the same. The volumetric flow rate at the entry and exit is $ V_n A $ Draw the outlet triangle with the blade velocity $ u_2 $ as one side. The relative velocity vector $ V_{r2} $ makes an angle {{params.beta2deg}} degrees with $ u_2 $. Also $ V_{n2} $ can be computed
  • $ \beta_1 = \tan^{-1} \frac{ V_1}{u_1} \implies \beta_1 = \tan^{-1} \frac{ {{params.V1}} }{ {{params.u1}} } \implies \beta_1 = {{params.beta1deg}} $
  • Volumetric flow rate: $ \dot{V} = 2 \pi R_1 b_1 V_{n1} = 2 \pi R_2 b_2 V_{n2} \implies V_{n2} = {{params.Vn2}} $ {{params.unitsSpeed}}
  • $ V_{t2} = u_2 - V_{n2} \, cot \beta_2 \implies V_{t2} = {{params.Vt2}} $ {{params.unitsSpeed}}
  • $ \alpha_2 = \tan^{-1} \frac{ V_{n2} }{ V_{n1} } \implies \alpha_2 = {{params.alpha2deg}} $
